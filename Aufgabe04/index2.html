What is "That '70s Show"

"That '70s Show" was an American TV show. It first aired on August 23, 1998. It was on TV till May 2006. Although it's basically a 90s/00s show, it's placed in the 70s hence the title.
It's about six teenagers in the 70s. Their names are: Eric Forman, Michael Kelso, Steven Hyde, Jacky Burkhardt, Donna Pinciotti and Fez (that's not actually his real name. It just stands for "Foreign exchange student")
There are also other characters on the show. Important to mention are Red and Kitty, Erics parents and Bob, Donnas Dad. There are also other relatives on the show, but they only appear every now and then.

Eric and Donna

They all live in Point Place, Wisconsin, a fictional city.
Eric and Donna are neighbors and best friends since Kindergarten. There's is more between them, what you can already notice in the very fisrt episode.
At first Donna hates Jacky, at that point Kelsos girlfriend, but they become best friends throughout the show. Later Jacky is dating Hide, but that's a whole other story.

The circle

As you probably know most actors who play teenagers are older than their characters. Mostly they are already in their twenties. Mila Kunis although, who starred Jacky on the show, was only 14 years old, when the first episode ran. So she was actually almost 2 years younger than her character and much younger than her co-workers.
Most of the time they're sitting in Erics basement. They especially like to sit in the so called "circle". What are they doing? Well, defenitely smoking more than cigarettes.
In general they are always at Eric's house. That's why his parents also have big roles on the show. Red is always treating Eric a little harsh, but in reality he loves his son. Kitty is always showing her love what can be a little bit too much sometimes. Most of the time, she's all over the place.

Trivia about the theme song

The theme song "In the streets" was written by a band called "Big Star". Since season 2 it's performed by the band "Cheap Trick"
"Big Star"'s cover ends with the phrase "We're all alright" which they took from their own song "Surrender".
At the end of both versions of the song somebody is yelling "Hello Wisconsin". The the original it is Danny Masterson, who played Hyde on the show. It's unknow who did it for the cover version.
